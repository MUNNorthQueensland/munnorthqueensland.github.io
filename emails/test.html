Dear partners of Model United Nations North Queensland,

We are writing to you with the exciting news that Model United Nations North Queensland is making a comeback in Term 4 of 2020! Despite the challenges presented by COVID-19, we have made the decision that holding a formal MUNNQ conference in Term 4 would be both feasible and safe given the current COVID-19 restrictions.

As you may already know, your Year 9-11 students have previously been involved in our MUNNQ Workshop in Term 3 of 2019. In this workshop the students were introduced to the fundamental principles of the United Nations (UN) and engaged in negotiating a resolution on the Syrian refugee crisis through a Model UN debate. We would like to extend an invitation for your students to attend a full-day, formal Model UN conference, hosted at Kirwan State High School on Friday 16th of October (Week 2 of Term 4). The theme of MUNNQ 2020 is “Our Planet, Our People”. It centres around the mutual responsibility that we all have as global citizens to positively contribute to peace and security around the world. Through this experience, your students will debate issues ranging from emerging environmental issues, climate refugees, potential solutions to the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic, and the humanitarian crisis in Yemen. Furthermore, they will develop their speaking, negotiation, and problem-solving abilities through a rigourous Model UN-style debate.

If your school is interested in having your Year 10-11 students attending this conference, please reply to our MUNNQ Communications email address along with the attached registration forms by 18th of September, Friday Week 10. We have allocated places as follows:

  • 20 students each from Pimlico State High School and Kirwan State High School;

  • 10 students each from William Ross State High School, Heatley Secondary College, Thuringowa State High School and Townsville State High School.

Lunch and snacks will be provided for the students and staff, so please indicate any dietary requirements on the registration form.

After the submission of your registrations, you and your students will be notified of their allocated countries and will be given pre-reading to complete before the conference.

We hope your students can attend, and we can’t wait to see them there!

What is Model United Nations North Queensland?
Model United Nations North Queensland (MUNNQ) is a student-led conference dedicated to introducing high school students in Years 9-11 to the dynamics and procedures of Model United Nations. In 2019, we made our debut with a one-day MUNNQ Workshop that introduced students to the Sustainable Development Goals, engaged them in negotiation on contemporary social conflicts and immersed them in current geopolitical conflicts, such as the Syrian refugee crisis.
Who we are
We are four Year 12 students from Pimlico and Kirwan State High Schools that are passionate about Model UN and sharing our passion with like-minded students. Our journey started with our attendance of CISSMUN X, a student-led Model UN conference, in Shanghai, China in 2019. After this amazing experience, we were inspired to give students living in North Queensland the opportunities usually reserved for students living in larger cities.
Our commitment to safety
Due to the current situation of the COVID-19 pandemic, MUNNQ has greatly considered the safety of all attendees. All adults at the conference will be required to socially distance and hand sanitizer will be made available for everyone. We would greatly appreciate it your students and attending teachers could bring personal water bottles. Students are also expected to be in school uniform.
